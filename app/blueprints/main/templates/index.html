Heart Health Essentials

Understanding Your Heart

The heart is a muscular organ about the size of a fist, located just behind and slightly left of the breastbone. It pumps blood through the network of arteries and veins called the cardiovascular system.

  • The heart has four chambers: two upper atria and two lower ventricles.
  • It beats about 100,000 times per day, pumping 5-6 quarts of blood each minute.
  • The heart's electrical system controls the rhythm of the heartbeat.

Key Heart Stats

  • Average adult heart rate: 60-100 beats per minute
  • Blood vessels length: 60,000 miles
  • Daily blood pump volume: About 2,000 gallons

Risk Factors

Common risk factors for heart disease include:

  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • Smoking
  • Obesity
  • Physical inactivity

Heart-Healthy Diet

A heart-healthy diet can help reduce the risk of heart disease and improve overall cardiovascular health. Key components include:

  • Fruits and vegetables: Aim for a variety of colors to get a wide range of nutrients.
  • Whole grains: Choose whole grain bread, pasta, and cereals over refined grains.
  • Lean proteins: Opt for fish, poultry, beans, and nuts.
  • Healthy fats: Include sources of omega-3 fatty acids and monounsaturated fats.
  • Limited sodium: Reduce salt intake to help control blood pressure.

Heart Health Tips

Balanced Diet

Incorporate a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins into your meals.

Regular Exercise

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week.

Stress Management

Practice relaxation techniques such as meditation, deep breathing, or yoga to reduce stress.

Quality Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night to support heart health and overall well-being.

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water throughout the day to support proper heart function and circulation.

Quit Smoking

If you smoke, quitting is one of the best things you can do for your heart health.

Heart Health Insights

Heart Attack Signs

  • Chest discomfort or pain
  • Upper body discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Nausea, lightheadedness, or cold sweats

Blood Pressure

Normal: Less than 120/80 mm Hg

Exercise Goal

150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week

Heart-Healthy Foods

Resting Heart Rate

Stress Relief
